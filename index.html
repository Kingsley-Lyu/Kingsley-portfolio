LLM-to-SQL Natural Language Interface NLP · LLM
Industry Partner Aspire2 Education — New Zealand's largest private tertiary provider

Commissioned by Aspire2 Education to solve a real operational problem: non-technical staff needed to query their Canvas LMS database without writing SQL. Built a guarded, schema-aware LLM-to-SQL pipeline over live Canvas data (87 tables, 149 FK relationships, 3.34M rows). User queries are grounded against the schema, routed through a shortcut or full generation path, then validated before read-only execution — preventing hallucinated or destructive queries. Achieved 86.7% accuracy on the Spider 1.0 benchmark (top 3 globally) and a 75% pass rate on Aspire2's real Canvas data after iterative optimisation with domain-specific prompt engineering.

NYC Poverty Spatial Regression — OLS, SEM & GWR Spatial Analysis

Modelled poverty rates across 297 NYC census tracts using OLS, Spatial Error Model, and Geographically Weighted Regression. Diagnosed spatial dependence in OLS residuals (Moran's I = 0.155), selected SEM via Robust LM tests, and demonstrated GWR's superiority with a local R² of 0.97 vs OLS's 0.80 — revealing spatially non-stationary relationships between race, employment, and poverty.

Wolfcamp Aquifer — Spatial Interpolation & Universal Kriging Spatial Analysis

Applied spatial interpolation methods to predict piezometric head at a target location in the Wolfcamp Aquifer, Texas. Fitted exponential and spherical variogram models after first-order trend removal, selected the spherical model via leave-one-out cross-validation (lower MSE and MSDR closer to 1), and produced a kriged prediction surface.

About Me

Based in Christchurch with a Master of Applied Data Science from the University of Canterbury. That project brought together database architecture, LLM prompt engineering, and close collaboration with a real institution to ship something operationally useful. I'm drawn to work where there's something real behind the numbers.
